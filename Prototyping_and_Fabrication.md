## ● Manufacturing Process
The robot was designed with practicality in mind, so all parts could be made using tools readily available in the lab. Most of the structural components are currently being 3D printed using PLA on a Raise3D E2 printer, which makes it easy to prototype, test, and quickly update designs without adding much cost. This approach allows for fast iteration as improvements are made. In cases where more precision or strength is needed, processes like laser cutting. Wherever possible, off-the-shelf components are incorporated to simplify the build and keep everything affordable.
● 3D printing (PLA, Raise3D E2)
● Laser cutting
● Off-the-shelf components
